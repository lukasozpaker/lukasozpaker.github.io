# Immigration George Santos Russia New Indictment

Our latest report on the fakenews.com

We have found fakenews to be spreading disinformation and plagiarizing journalists material. A source provided us with the computer of the person behind this website, here’s what we found.

Article 1:

Upon inspection of the computer, we found the first article to be a plagiarized from a CNN article by the name of “First on CNN: Rep. George Santos charged by Justice Department in federal probe” by journalists Mark Morales, Evan Perez and Gregory Krieg of CNN. We have proof that they took the article and had an AI rephrase it to pass it off as their own work, we believe this to be functionally the same as direct plagiarism, but more insidious as a bias was inserted into the rephrasing.

Another concerning revelation about this is that this was a First on CNN story which the Journalists worked hard to get out as fast as they could, other news organizations use writers that work at human speed but Fake News was able to get this article out moments after CNN because of the speed of AI tools.

Additionally there is clear evidence of bias in the rephrasing which wasn’t present in the original CNN article. For example, “Democrats are grappling with the embarrassment of not exposing Santos' alleged misconduct sooner.”

The original CNN article can be found here: First on CNN: Rep. George Santos charged by Justice Department in federal probe

Article 2:

Our fact checkers have determined that the video contained in this article is completely false, created using a generative AI tool named Kapwing and that the article itself was generated by OpenAI’s ChatGPT. AI Video generation tools are still relatively crude, and as you can see in the video, Kapwing places a watermark on this content, however, as these models get better, it will become increasing difficult to distinguish real from fake.

While disinformation has been a concern over the past few years, the speed at which these AI models are able to pump out seemingly credible and well spoken text from simple prompts has greatly increased the threat and credibility of misinformation on social media.

Check out the tool(s) yourself: Kapwing Video Creator ChatGPT

Article 3:

After checking with the former President’s staff we have determined that the audio and video to be fake. While somewhat crude, the audio of Obama confused some readers on social media, we have confirmed that it is fake and was created by a Text-to-Speech tool called TopMediAI. Additionally, the accompanying article was created using text generation by Google Bard and the image was created by Bing Image Creator.

It is also worth discussing consent here, the former President did not consent to having his voice processed by an AI model that can uses his likeness to promote anything the user wants.

Check out the tool(s) yourself: Google Bard TopMediAI Bing Image Creator

Article 4:

We have determined that the claim that the two pieces of art were created by Leonardo Da Vinci to be false. A source confirmed that the images were indeed created by Dall-E 2, an image generation tool that uses diffusion to convert a text prompt into an image.

Here consent isn’t as much of an issue as the work of Da Vinci is in the public domain, but it is still a very relevant moral and ethical question and artists like Da Vinci aren’t alive to defend their work. It is also worth noting these models can and has been used to create work in the style of contemporary artists, however, Da Vinci is a very large name and very recognizable so it served the example well. AI art is a very real legal gray area with many lawsuits currently pending [1].

Check out the tool(s) yourself: Dall-E 2 Image Creator

Article 5:

We have determined that the claim that these two Jazz freestyles were created by Duke Ellington to be fake. This model is concerning because it clearly draws inspiration from Duke Ellington and John Coltrane, neither of whom consented to their expression in the form of music being used to train a model on which can pump out an infinite amount of Jazz they likely would have been against, as Jazz to these musicians was a form of human expression.

It can be very hard or even impossible to identity AI generated content BUT you can look for:

  • Some Image Generation tools like Bing place watermarks on their images
  • Current Text Generation models use relatively simple sentences structures often measured by perpexlity which can be a tell, but is not a definitive way of identifying AI generated text as tools that test perplexity often have many false positives and false negatives.
